My Story

Built from
Nothing.

This is not a polished origin story. It is the unfiltered account of a 19-year-old from Hyderabad who lost everything early — and built everything back, alone, one difficult day at a time.

I

My Roots

I was born in Hyderabad into a modest family — the kind where love was never lacking but resources often were. My mother passed away early, leaving a void that no amount of time fully fills. My father, unable to provide the emotional or financial scaffolding a child needs, meant I learned the weight of independence before most kids learn long division.

Hyderabad raised me: its chaos, its heat, its contradictions, its relentless energy. That city taught me that survival isn’t passive — you must go out and claim it every single morning.

II

The Fight Begins

In 7th grade, a financial crisis didn’t just knock on our door — it kicked it down. School became a luxury I couldn’t afford. I left, found work in a restaurant, and started paying for my own survival. I moved between hostels, switching schools when I could, stitching together an education from whatever fragments were available.

Most people at that age worry about homework deadlines. I was worrying about whether I could eat that night. But here is what those years gave me that no curriculum could: the absolute, bone-deep certainty that I would not break.

I was a kid holding two impossible things at once — poverty and ambition. I chose to keep both.

“I was a kid holding two impossible things at once — poverty and ambition. I chose to keep both.”

III

Self-Made Path

I found open schooling — a route most people dismiss, but one I treated like a lifeline. I completed an ITI diploma course with distinction honours through sheer determination, studying whenever I could carve out the time between shifts.

Eventually, the path pointed toward law. NLUD — one of the top national law universities in India. I studied for it, sat for it, and got in. I paid my first-year fees entirely on my own, through part-time jobs. No safety net. Just hustle and the refusal to let logistics be the reason my dream dies.

IV

Conquering Delhi

Delhi hit me like a wall. A new city, a new climate, isolation, and a chronic stomach illness worsened by the pollution. I missed two consecutive weeks of classes. I was failing subjects. The mental weight was real and heavy.

And still — I kept fighting. I caught up. I rebuilt. Because that’s the only playbook I know. You fall, you note why, you stand back up, and you move forward. Every. Single. Time.

V

My Drive & Ambitions

I am a certified distinction holder. A writer of 90+ essays. A builder of AI tools, dashboards, and games. A person who left formal school in 7th grade and is now studying at a national law university. I handle everything — independently, without complaint.

My target is the highest-paying job in corporate law, blended with technology and creativity. The intersection of law, technology, and creativity is where I am staking my career. Three years remain at NLUD. The runway is short. The ambition is not.

VI

Looking Ahead

Freedom is not something you are given. It is something you earn, day by day, through work that no one sees and costs that no one else pays. I have been earning mine since I was in 7th grade.

I’m not interested in sympathy. I’m interested in results — until my story becomes something that makes the next kid from a difficult background think: if he did it from there, I can do it from here.

“No safety net. No shortcut. No backup plan.
Just the absolute refusal to stay down.”

— Dhananjay Chouhan, 19 · Hyderabad → Delhi
